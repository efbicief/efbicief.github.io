CS130 - Mathematics for Computer Scientists 1

  • Module Leader: Dmitri Chistikov (include "CS130" in email subject line if contacting)
  • Lecture notes from previous years: Here
  • Pre-recorded lectures made available on Echo360 [Moodle]
  • Online live lectures on Teams [Tabula] every Wednesday at 10:00
  • Seminars timetabled once a week, starting week 3 (week starting 2020-10-19)
    • Assessment
      Coursework (20%, all submission deadlines on Monday at 12noon):
    • 1% for initial “dry run” of peer feedback loop (weeks 2 and 3)
    • 3% formative assignment submissions (deadlines in weeks 4, 6, 8) [1% for each]
    • 6% engagement with peer feedback (deadlines in weeks 5, 7, 9) [2% for each, conditional on submission of assignment]
    • 10% final problem sheet (deadline in week 10)
    • Exam (80%): in Term 3
    • Problem sets will be made available here
    Reading list
    • Proofs and Fundamentals by Ethan D. Bloch
    • Book of Proof by Richard Hammack
    • “Discrete mathematics for computer scientists” by J.K. Truss (available from Warwick library)
    • “Discrete mathematics and its applications” by Kenneth H. Rosen (available from Warwick library)
    • “Discrete mathematics” by Kenneth A. Ross, Charles R. B. Wright (available from Warwick library)

CS132 - Computer Organisation & Architecture

  • Module Leader: Matthew Leake
  • Pre-recorded lectures and lecture notes available on module webpage
  • Online live lectures on Teams [Tabula] every Thurdsay at 10:00
  • Labs / workshops in DCS building bi-weekly [Tabula], with a weekly Teams event for self-isolating / remote students
    • Lecture Topics
    • Week 1: Introduction
    • Week 2: Data Representation
    • Week 3-4: Digital Logic
    • Week 5: Assembler
    • Week 6: Memory Systems
    • Week 7: I/O Mechanisms
    • Week 8-10: Processor Architecture
    Labs Topics
    • Lab 1: Getting Started with C, Data types, Operators (Weeks 2-3)
    • Lab 2: Control Flow, Control Structures, Functions (Weeks 4-5)
    • Lab 3: Pointers, Arrays, Strings (Weeks 6-7)
    • Lab 4: Memory Management, Structures, User input and File I/O (Weeks 8-9)
    • Lab Troubleshooting (Week 10, Online)
    Assessment
    • Coursework 1 (15%), Set week 4, Due Term 1 Tuesday week 9 @ noon
    • Coursework 2 (25%), Set week 8, Due Term 2 Thurdsay week 3 @ noon
    • Exam (60%) in term 3
    Reading List
    • Structured Computer Organization by Andrew S. Tanenbaum; Todd Austin (Essential)
    • Principles of Computer Hardware by Alan Clements (Essential)
    • C for Java Programmers by Tomasz Müldner
    • The C Programming Language (AKA The C Bible) by Brian W. Kernighan; Dennis M. Ritchie
  • Additional resources: Here
